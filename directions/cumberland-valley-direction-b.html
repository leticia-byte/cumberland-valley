Not On The Clock

"Your pet's case isn't on a clock." The site is built to feel like the Tuesday-night 9pm appointment — lamplight, no rush, someone still there after everyone else has closed. Editorial, dark, quiet.

The logo's navy, pushed one stop deeper into night. "Keeping care close to home" sets in lamp-amber italic — the one warm thing in a cold frame. The story the motion tells: the amber glow behind the headline breathes on a 9-second cycle, like a lamp someone left on. Nothing else moves. The Tuesday-10pm badge sits on the photo because it's the practice's hardest-to-copy fact.

The surprise is Lamp. The logo gives us navy and gray and nothing else — which is exactly the problem with the current site: it's cold. One amber, used only for the phone CTA and one italic phrase, turns the whole navy system from "clinic" into "someone's still here."

Typography

Headings — Newsreader 300
We take as long as the visit needs.

A light serif with real optical sizing. It reads like a book, not a brochure — which is the point. This practice doesn't shout.

Body — Karla 400 / 17px / 1.75

Some visits are quick, some aren't. Either way, your pet's case isn't on a clock. We're here to answer your questions, and to make decisions with you, not for you.

Karla is a grotesque with slightly quirky letterforms — warm without going soft. Body sits at 17px minimum, 1.75 line-height, for the older readers.

Rationale

This is the direction that takes Dr. Dolan's own words most literally. The risk: dark sites are harder for older, low-vision users, so every contrast ratio has to clear AA and the dark stretches stay short. The reward: nobody in the Hagerstown market looks like this. Every competitor is white-and-blue — and so, right now, is Cumberland Valley.
