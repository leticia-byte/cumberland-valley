What Community-Based Veterinary Care Looks Like

Three things we hold to, every visit.

01

We give you options, not ultimatums.

Not every pet owner can afford every test, every procedure, or every referral. We help you understand what's possible, what matters most for your dog or cat, and how to make it fit for you.

We keep care close to home.

We do our best to provide the majority of your dog or cat's care in-house. Surgery, dentistry, diagnostics, and most complex cases for pets can be handled here.

Preventive & Wellness Care

Frequently Asked Questions About Cumberland Valley Veterinary Clinic

How much does a typical vet visit cost?

The cost of a vet visit depends on what your pet needs. A typical wellness exam at Cumberland Valley includes a head-to-tail physical exam, any vaccinations due, and time for your questions. More complex or sick visits may involve diagnostics like bloodwork or imaging, which come with additional costs. We'll always explain what we recommend and why, walk through the options with you, and give you an estimate before anything moves forward. If a treatment plan feels out of reach, tell us. We'll help you figure out what's possible for your pet.

Is Cumberland Valley independently owned?

Yes. Cumberland Valley Veterinary Clinic has been privately owned since [VERIFY: YEAR], when Dr. Jen Dolan bought the practice. Independent ownership means our decisions stay local. That includes who we hire, what equipment we invest in, how long we spend with each patient, and what we charge. We're one of the last few independent veterinary practices in the Hagerstown area, and Dr. Dolan plans to keep it that way. When you come to Cumberland Valley, you're supporting a small business that's grounded in this community.

What kinds of surgery do you perform at your veterinary clinic?

Cumberland Valley performs a range of surgeries in-house, including soft-tissue procedures, CCL repair for orthopedic cases, mass removals, spay and neuter, and dental surgery. Our team monitors every patient continuously from pre-op through recovery, so your dog or cat is never left unwatched during any part of the procedure. Our surgical menu is broad enough that most of what your pet needs can be handled here, without a referral to a specialist an hour away. If we do recommend a referral, we'll explain why and help coordinate the appointment.

What are your hours?

Cumberland Valley is open Monday through Saturday for regular appointments, with extended Tuesday evening hours until 10 p.m. Our Pet Resort has its own schedule for boarding drop-offs and pickups throughout the week. For same-day appointments call (301) 739-3121.

Do you have evening or after-work appointments?

Yes. Cumberland Valley stays open Tuesday evenings until 10 p.m. specifically for families who can't make it to a veterinarian during a standard workday. Between school pickups, shift work, and everything else on a family's calendar, a 9-to-5 schedule leaves too many pets waiting for a visit that should happen sooner. Our Tuesday evening appointments are the same kind of visit you'd get during the day: the same doctors, the same attention, the same unrushed conversation. To book an evening appointment, call (301) 739-3121.

What should I look for when choosing a veterinarian?

A good veterinarian listens more than they lecture, takes time with both you and your pet, walks you through the options instead of handing you ultimatums, and treats your family like neighbors rather than transactions. Look for a practice where the same team has stayed together for years. That kind of continuity usually shows up in the details: how well they remember your pet, how carefully they explain what they're seeing, and how they handle the hard conversations. At Cumberland Valley, that's what we've been building for over thirty years.
