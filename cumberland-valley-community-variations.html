Variation A · Signpost

The towns as a literal signpost

Each town becomes an arrow-shaped sign plate stacked on a post — the rural crossroads sign you'd actually pass driving in from Boonsboro. Playful but grounded, and it turns a plain list into the one image that says "people drive here." Closes on Alie's line, set as the payoff.

Variation B · Map Field

Hagerstown at the centre, towns radiating out

Navy field with concentric rings rippling out from a white "Hagerstown, MD" hub — a map without being a map. The towns sit as pills around it. This is closest to what's on the homepage now, but with the radius made visible: you can feel the catchment area rather than just read it.

Variation C · Split Ledger

Statement left, towns as a ruled list right

Copy and CTA on oat at left; the towns run as a ruled ledger on a stone-textured navy panel at right, each with its drive distance. The most information-dense of the three — and the drive times quietly do the arguing that "worth the drive" makes explicit.
