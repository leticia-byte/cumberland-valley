The surprise is Brick. It's the only color that appears in an otherwise black-and-white world, and it's reserved for human voice — pull-quotes, staff names, the words people actually said. Color = a person talking. Nothing else gets it.

Typography

Headings — Instrument Serif
Many of them have been here longer than she has.

A high-contrast display serif with a journalistic feel — long-form magazine, not clinic brochure. It signals that the page is going to tell you something, not sell you something.

Body — Inter Tight 400 / 17px / 1.75

Being a small business means you'll see a lot of familiar faces here. Many of our doctors, technicians, and receptionists have been at Cumberland Valley longer than Dr. Dolan has.

Inter Tight keeps the caption-and-credit voice compact under photos while staying at 17px for body — the accessibility floor holds.

Rationale

This one comes from outside veterinary design entirely — it's built on the visual language of long-form local journalism. It's the direction that most directly answers "how do we prove independent matters?" without ever arguing the point: you show the people, you show how long they've stayed, and you let a 22-year text message do the work that a value-prop bullet can't. The catch: it lives or dies on real photography, and right now we have none.
