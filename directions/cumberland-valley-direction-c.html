Plain Spoken

No hero photo. No mood. The words are the hero, set enormous, and the phone number is the biggest thing on the page. This direction takes the audience constraint — older, blue-collar, farming, some low-vision — and makes it the aesthetic instead of a compromise.

Everything is oversized, high-contrast, and unmissable. The CTA has a hard navy drop-shadow — it looks like a physical button. The story the motion tells: one 3px rule draws itself left-to-right under the nav on load, like a line ruled in a ledger, and then the page stops moving. That's the entire animation budget. Zero parallax. Zero fade-ins on body copy.

Typography

Headings — Archivo 800, tight
We give you options, not ultimatums.

Archivo Black-adjacent, tracked tight. It's a signage face — DMV, feed store, county fair. It has no opinion about being tasteful, which is exactly right for a practice that says "I just want something functional."

Body — Source Serif 4 / 19px / 1.7

Not every pet owner can afford every test, every procedure, or every referral. We help you understand what's possible, what matters most for your dog or cat, and how to make it fit for you.

Body copy runs at 19px — two full steps above the fleet default. Serif body at large sizes reads faster for older eyes than sans does.

Rationale

Dr. Dolan told you exactly what she wanted: "I just want something that is functional and works. I don't have a lot of high— just something that's nice." Most directions treat that as a floor to clear. This one treats it as the brief. It also solves the hardest constraint in the thesis — the CTA is a phone call, and here the phone number is genuinely, physically the biggest element on every screen. The risk: it may read as under-designed to Dr. Dolan, who is about to go compare her site to Funkstown's. That's a conversation worth having.
