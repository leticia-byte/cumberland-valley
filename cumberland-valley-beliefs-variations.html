Variation A · Editorial Cards

Three cards, photo-led

Each belief becomes a white card on the oat background: photo on top with a small numbered chip, headline and copy beneath. Light, familiar, and it matches the Services grid further down the page — the most cohesive with what's already built, and the easiest to scan.

What Community-Based Veterinary Care Looks Like

01

We give you options, not ultimatums.

Not every pet owner can afford every test, every procedure, or every referral. We help you understand what's possible, what matters most for your dog or cat, and how to make it fit for you.

02

We keep care close to home.

We do our best to provide the majority of your dog or cat's care in-house. Surgery, dentistry, diagnostics, and most complex cases for pets can be handled here.

03

We take as long as the visit needs.

Some visits are quick, some aren't. Either way, your pet's case isn't on a clock. We're here to answer your questions, and to make decisions with you, not for you.

Variation B · Alternating Bands

Full-bleed, cinematic

Three edge-to-edge bands on navy, photo and text swapping sides as you scroll. The photos run huge — half the screen each — so the emotion carries. This is the most immersive option and the biggest tonal shift from the rest of the page; it turns the beliefs into the emotional centre of the homepage.

Variation C · Quiet List

Type-led, photo on hover

The beliefs are set as a plain ruled list — big serif statements, nothing shouting. The photo only appears as a small thumbnail when you hover a row. It's the most restrained and the most "unhurried," which is arguably the most honest expression of "your pet's case isn't on a clock."

Note: hover-only photos don't exist on touch devices — on mobile this becomes a clean text list (photos hidden).
