## The 3 design-load-bearing observations

1. **Hero headline:** "Hagerstown's veterinarian, keeping care close to home."
   Sub: "Cumberland Valley Veterinary Clinic has been Hagerstown's privately owned, multi-doctor veterinary practice and pet resort for over 30 years."
   CTA is a **phone number**, not a booking form — Dr. Dolan explicitly refuses online self-scheduling. Every CTA on this page is `Call (301) 739-3121`. Design must make a phone number feel like the most natural action on the page, not a fallback.

2. **Voice signal:** Plain, unhurried, neighborly, zero polish. Dr. Dolan on what she wants people to feel: *"that my practice is like a family and we care about your pet like they're our own."* On what the site should do: *"I just want something that is functional and works. I don't have a lot of high— just something that's nice."* Audience is heavily blue-collar, farming, and geriatric — western Maryland, many who **won't drive 40 minutes over the mountain** to a specialist. That's an accessibility constraint, not a style preference: large type, high contrast, subtle-or-no animation, mobile-first (the current site is not phone friendly and she named that).

3. **Core tension:** **Independent vs. corporate — and "close to home" as both a promise and a geography.**
   Cumberland Valley is one of the last independent multi-doctor practices in Washington County. Nobody else in the Hagerstown market leads with that. The copy's job is conversion, not ranking (they already rank #1 for most local terms). So the scroll has to resolve: *why does independent actually matter to me and my dog?* → answer: options not ultimatums, care stays in-house, the visit isn't on a clock, and the same faces have been here 20+ years.

Two stained-glass windows of that logo still hang in the clinic. → This is the single richest visual artifact available. Photos requested from Dr. Dolan.
