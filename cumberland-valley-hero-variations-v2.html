Concept 1 · Masonry

The wall becomes the layout

The stone isn't behind anything — it is the grid. The hero is built as a dry-stack masonry field, and content is mortared into it: a photo window cut on the right, the headline set into a recessed panel on the left, the phone number laid in as a navy capstone, a hearth-amber "New patients" block beside it. It reads like the copy was built into the wall the way the clinic was rebuilt around the one that survived.

Stone role: structural grid + window frames. Photography: one hero image, framed as a stone window. Personality: solid, hand-built, permanent.

Concept 2 · Threshold

Standing at the wall, looking in

A full-bleed photograph seen over a real stone wall. The wall's ragged top edge is a genuine die-cut mask — the photo sits behind the masonry, so you're looking across the wall into the practice, exactly the vantage the logo draws. The headline lives in the open sky above the wall line; the phone CTA rests on the stone cap like something set down on it. This is the most cinematic and the most photography-forward.

Stone role: die-cut foreground plane (real mask, not overlay). Photography: full-bleed landscape or facility shot. Personality: warm, arrival, "worth the drive."

Concept 3 · Keystone

The wall as a load-bearing column

The premium, gallery-quiet option. Nearly all oat-white space, with a single stone column running floor-to-ceiling as a load-bearing pillar — capped like real masonry, with the mark's dog sitting at its base. The serif headline interlocks with the column edge so the type and the stone share a seam. Restrained, confident, and the easiest to keep pristine on mobile and for older eyes.

Stone role: architectural column / anchor. Photography: none required — type-forward. Personality: established, calm, unrushed.
